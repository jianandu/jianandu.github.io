I am a first-year PhD student in Political Science at [Northwestern University](https://polisci.northwestern.edu/), where I study comparative politics with a regional focus on China.

## Research Interests

My research examines how **premodern beliefs**—particularly religious and supernatural thinking—continue to shape **bureaucratic behaviors** in modern authoritarian states. I am drawn to questions at the intersection of:

- **Chinese politics**: How do historical cultural legacies influence contemporary governance?
- **Bureaucratic politics**: How do officials navigate uncertainty, competition, and accountability?
- **Religion and institutions**: How do informal beliefs and practices interact with formal institutional structures?

I employ **quantitative and computational methods** to study these questions, drawing on original datasets and novel empirical strategies.

## Background

Before coming to Northwestern, I received my M.A. in Political Science from [Columbia University](https://polisci.columbia.edu/) and my B.A. in Translation from [Beijing Foreign Studies University](http://www.bfsu.edu.cn/).
